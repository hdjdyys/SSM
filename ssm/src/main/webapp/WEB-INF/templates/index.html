Admit it, you are also a member of the "Appearance Association". In fact, you are constantly judging whether a face looks good or not. From the perspective of evolution, we know that only faces that can indicate a certain degree of evolutionary significance are defined as good-looking (Little, Jones, DeBruine, 2011)。 That is to say, good-looking or not is a collection of acquired concepts, which abstractly summarizes all the features people like. If you want to understand the persistence of human face attraction, let's start with a scene: today is your first day at work, so how long do you think it will take you to judge whether a new colleague's face looks good or not? The answer seems to vary from person to person. But scientists found through experiments that a person can judge the attractiveness of a face very quickly. For example, Perrett Doctors and colleagues (2001) found that even if a face is only presented for tens of milliseconds, that is, less than 0.1 seconds, one can judge whether the face looks good or not in such a short time. Dr. will and Dr. Todorov (2006) also further proved this point. Such an effective and automatic beauty judgment mechanism is embedded in our brain. When judging faces, many brain regions work together. For example, fusiform Q Face area) and superior temporal sulcus (STS) can comprehensively process face information; The insula °, an area involving the reward system, will give us a sense of being rewarded when we see beautiful faces, which will promote us to communicate with people who have beautiful faces (here it can be understood as having certain excellent characteristics). Why did you judge so quickly? Let's suppose such a scenario: as primitive people, we are hunting. At this time, one person suggested to join us in hunting, and then divide the prey in proportion. So, do you choose to let them join, or do you choose to keep the original number of people in the team? At this moment, we will judge by each other's faces. Some people in the team will say that we have to judge whether this person can be trusted, after all, unreliable people may harm us; Others will say that we should judge whether the person's cooperation ability is good or not, because only a united team can do complex things well; Some people even say that we should judge whether the person is healthy, strong or not. If he is not strong, he may not be able to hunt well. These features can actually be found on the face. Through experiments and statistical analysis, Dr. Todorov found that the characteristics of many faces are closely linked. The beauty of a face is related to its trustworthiness and even its health. In this way, judging the beauty of a face can more or less predict the social characteristics of the other person. Since so many characteristics are crucial in the first contact, it is quick and efficient to judge whether it looks good or not. Then is it not a matter of seeking distance without judging by the degree of attractiveness? The "appearance association" still has some truth.
